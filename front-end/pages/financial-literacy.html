Essential Financial Concepts

Master these fundamental ideas to make better financial decisions

Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods. It's often called "interest on interest" and can help savings grow faster over time.

Example: If you invest ₹10,000 at 8% annual interest, in 10 years you'll have ₹21,589 without adding any more money.

Asset allocation is an investment strategy that aims to balance risk and reward by dividing assets among major categories such as stocks, bonds, and cash based on your goals, risk tolerance, and investment timeframe.

Example: A young investor might allocate 80% to stocks and 20% to bonds, while someone nearing retirement might choose 40% stocks and 60% bonds.

Diversification is a risk management strategy that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio to minimize the impact of any single security or risk factor.

Example: Instead of investing only in technology stocks, a diversified portfolio might include stocks from different sectors, bonds, real estate, and international investments.

The time value of money is the concept that money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future due to its potential earning capacity through investment or interest.

Example: ₹10,000 today is worth more than ₹10,000 in 5 years because you can invest today's money and earn interest or returns.
